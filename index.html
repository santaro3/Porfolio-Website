Dakkota Integrated Systems - Data & Quality Analyst

Dakkota Integrated Systems is the manufacturer for the instrument panels for the Chrysler Pacfica and Dodge Caravan. I was fortunate enough to be at Dakkota during the launch of the 2017 Chrysler Pacifica and was able to be a part of the huge changes made to the manufacturing process. At Dakkota I monitored the torques of the torque guns on the assembly line as well as provided analysis reports for upper management using Crystal Reports and their MSSQL database. This involved performing data analysis on data in large Excel spreadsheets using Visual Basic for Applications (VBA). Much of the data analysis tasks I did at Dakkota I automated using VBA allowing the future co-ops to spend more time on the assembly line troubleshooting issues with the torque guns rather than creating reports.

Dakkota was also my first introduction to manufacturing quality control and the numerous tools and processes corporations use to ensure the highest quality in their products and services. My tasks primarily involved applying Sigma Six tools (Capability Analysis, DMAIC, DFMEA, etc.), Kaizens and lean manufacturing principles to report and resolve qualify defects on any instrument panels that were ready to be shipped to the next manufacturing plant. Throughout the co-op I solidified my understanding of quality management and the various tools used to optimize the manufacturing and design process.

FCA - Resident Powertrain Engineer

The Windsor Assembly Plant is the largest manufacturing plant in Windsor and a leader in automotive innovation. I had the privilege of being a powertrain resident engineer at the Windsor Assembly Plant. At the plant I worked with talented teams of engineers to accomplish goals and solve manufacturing issues with very strict deadlines. My duties ranged from testing new line products and parts to analyzing data from the PLC machines on the assembly line. My daily activities were centered around coordinating the various Powertrain engineering teams to quickly diagnose and solve manufacturing defects or issues on the assembly line.

During my time at FCA I learned the physical makeup and operation of many of the automotive systems ranging from the coolant system, to the electronic braking systems. I was also exposed to modern vehicle bus architectures and networks such as automotive ethernet, Flexray and CAN-LIN bus architectures. With a fully illustrated high-level view of automobiles in hand I was able to quickly diagnose and narrow down the root causes of issues and defects found on vehicles coming off the production line.

To diagnose issues I frequently made use of the "Vector" product suite using tools like CANoe and CANape to perform ECU system tests, monitoring, and calibrations. I was also involved in a project involving ECU specification design for one of FCA Tier 1 suppliers in which I learned about DBC and LDF files and how they are used to design ECUs. I gained experience with ECUC files and how they provide a detailed and arguably more comprehensive view of the software specifications in ECUs. This project was the start to my long journey into AUTOSAR and the new software design methodology it brings with it.

Enwin - Sr. Electrical Engineering Student

Enwin Utilites is the power distribution company for Windsor Ontario. They deliver power to thousands of customers around the Windsor region including hundreds of manufacturing plants. As a co-op student at Enwin I worked with a talented team to analyze the harmonics and quality of electricity going to our industrial-sized customers. Using powerful CAD tools I worked alongside PhD and Master's level engineers to determine concerning and growing harmonic oscillations in the power distribution network as well as identify any potentially troubling amounts of reactance in the network. Using tools such as ArcGIS and powerful SQL queries I was able to quickly analyze thousands of distribution transformers to determine which transformers were overloaded and unhealthy and how Enwin could re-route power to improve the power grid's stability, efficiency and performance.

Enwin provided me with the opportunities to develop a fundamental understanding of city-sized power distribution networks from 510V three phase transformers to industrial SCADA switches. During my time at Enwin I was also fortunate enough to be given the opportunity to develop new software functionality for their distribution network analysis CAD program. "Cyme" is the CAD tool that Enwin uses to analyze harmonics in the power grid, I was responsible for creating Python scripts for the Cyme specialists at Enwin to speed up and expand their analysis of the power grid. I then generalized their analyses and developed health reports of the grid (by leveraging Enwin's MSSQL-PHP backend) and presented them to the upper management executives at Enwin.
